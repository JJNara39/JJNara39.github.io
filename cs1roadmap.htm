Case Study Roadmap

Cyclistic Bike Share Analysis

In this case study, I was asked to look at the previous 12 months of data provided by this fictitious company Cyclistic. After doing so, and after making a few creative changes to my analysis to make it work with the limitations offered by Microsoft Excel. Ideally, as part of the Case Study Roadmap, I went through each step of the Data Analytics process, which are: ask, prepare, process, analyze, prepare, act. Each step requires a deliverable.

·         Ask

o   Deliverable – A clear statement of the business task.

§  Our task is to find out how casual and annual drivers differ. Once we do, we can find out how to convert more casual drivers and see if digital media can play a part.

·         Prepare

o   Deliverable – A description of all data sources used.

§  I got my dataset from https://divvy-tripdata.s3.amazonaws.com/index.html, a reliable source to obtain data.

§  The data has been made available by Motivate International Inc. under the license provided at https://www.divvybikes.com/data-license-agreement; a license aids in solidifying the authenticity of this data.

·         Process

o   Deliverable - Documentation of any cleaning or manipulation of data (aka a changelog)

§  August 31, 2021 2:05pm – 2:14pm: converted each monthly csv file to excel workbook

§  August 31, 2021 2:48pm – 3:07pm: created ride_length column and calculated all entries as “=’ended_at’ – ‘started_at’”, changed formatting to HH:MM:SS

§  August 31, 2021 4:42pm – 4:49pm: created day_of_week column and used WEEKDAY command and formatted as Day of Week

·         Analyze

o   Deliverable - A summary of your analysis

§  With the restrictions offered by my computer, I was unable to merge the files to find seasonal relations. Instead I made pivot tables and visualizations for all 12 months, so my conclusions may be a little different from what you would expect. In all months casual users use Bikes more than members. These will influence recommendations given in the “act” step.

 

 

·         Share

o   Deliverable – Supporting visualizations and key findings

o   September Findings

o   March Findings

o   I found that more casuals used bikes, even 6 months apart, so it is imperative that we find ways to make these more exciting to bring in more members.

o   As you can see, even 6 months apart, weekends are among the most popular days, regardless of users, so it is also very important to incentivize weekday usage so we can increase the numbers those days.

·         Act

o   Deliverable – Your top three recommendations based on your analysis

§  Here is what I have noticed, there are way more casuals than members on days during the week, during any time frame. Therefore, my first recommendation: incentivize weekday usage.

§  Secondly there are more casuals than members, always, so there comes my second recommendation: make membership more rewarding.

§  Lastly, my data is limited, I do not see how much casuals pay for their bikes vs. how much members pay in membership. My data is lacking, so here is my third recommendation: use surveys and figure out what is keeping people from converting.

In summary, this was my very first case study. It was self-paced and it was not set to be graded so I had a little bit of freedom in regards to how I used this data. The problems I have found with this data is that price for membership would be good to know, since tiny edits can always be made to make them more enticing for more people. Also alternatives would be greatly appreciated, even though I cant do seasonal or all year analysis, it would be simpler to do them on a month-by-month basis. I know this summary may be cut and dry but hopefully with more case studies they will slowly grow into better summaries.
